Proposal Generator Refactor — Option A (Energy Supply Savings Only, Term-Specific Quotes)
Context

Proposals are sales collateral, not admin reports.

Audience: non-expert business decision-makers.

Inputs available: customer bill(s) + supplier quotes (client uplifted prices per term) + branded PDF generation.

Objective

Refactor Proposal Generator so that:

every quote clearly shows estimated savings (R$) over its own contract term

the proposal tells a clear financial story: today vs best option vs alternatives

output improves close probability via: best option framing + term effect + monthly framing

adds a trust-building line: “Baseado no seu histórico de consumo — sem compromisso.”

REQUIRED IMPLEMENTATION (Option A)
1) Baseline Definition (Compared-to-what)

All savings are compared against the client’s current bill-derived baseline for the energy supply component only.

Baseline inputs (from bill OCR / bill parsing)

baseline_consumption_mwh_12m

Prefer last 12 months actual; if fewer months available, annualise.

baseline_energy_supply_cost_12m

Energy supply component only (exclude regulated charges not comparable to the quote).

Baseline transparency line (must appear in PDF once)

“Comparação baseada na parcela de fornecimento de energia das suas faturas atuais (últimos 12 meses).”

If annualised:

“Estimativa baseada no histórico disponível de faturas (anualizado).”

2) Quote Data Model (Term-specific pricing is mandatory)

Each quote is term-specific. Therefore each quote row must include its own term and its own client price.

Quote inputs (per quote)

supplier_name

energy_type (convencional / incentivada)

term_months (e.g., 24 / 36 / 60)

client_price_r$/mwh (uplifted — client-facing)

indexation_type (display only; do not model indexation)

valid_until (optional, display if available)

3) Savings Math (Per quote, per term — Mandatory)

Use only:

current cost = bill-based baseline

proposed cost = client price (uplifted)

Let:

MWh_annual = baseline_consumption_mwh_12m

baseline_cost_12m = baseline_energy_supply_cost_12m

Compute for each quote:

Proposed annual energy supply cost

proposed_cost_12m = MWh_annual × client_price_r$/mwh

Annual savings (energy supply only)

savings_12m = baseline_cost_12m – proposed_cost_12m

Total savings over quote term

savings_total = savings_12m × (term_months / 12)

Average monthly savings over term

savings_monthly_avg = savings_total / term_months

Guardrails

If savings_total < 0, label as:

“Aumento estimado no período: R$ X”

No % savings. Only R$.

Always include fixed footnote in PDF:

“Economia estimada apenas na parcela de fornecimento de energia, baseada no consumo dos últimos 12 meses. Valores podem variar conforme perfil e condições contratuais.”

SALES “INFLATION” (Legit framing — Required)
A) Headline uses Best Option (term-specific)

Choose the “Best Option” as the quote with the highest positive savings_total.

Executive Summary headline:

“Potencial de economia estimada de até R$ {BEST_SAVINGS_TOTAL} no prazo de {BEST_TERM_MONTHS} meses.”

Sub-line:

“Equivalente a ~R$ {BEST_MONTHLY_AVG} por mês (média no período).”

Add trust line (below sub-line):

“Baseado no seu histórico de consumo — sem compromisso.”

B) Term effect is shown explicitly (24/36/60)

Because prices differ by term, the table must clearly communicate tradeoffs.

Add a short line above the table:

“As ofertas variam por prazo (24/36/60 meses). A economia total reflete o prazo escolhido.”

C) Monthly + Over-term framing (every quote row)

Each quote must show:

R$ no período (prazo) → savings_total

R$ / mês (média) → savings_monthly_avg

PDF LAYOUT (Updated)
Section A — Executive Summary

Headline: “Potencial de economia estimada de até R$ X no prazo de Y meses”

Sub-line: “~R$ Z por mês (média no período)”

Trust line: “Baseado no seu histórico de consumo — sem compromisso.”

One scope line (small):

“Comparação baseada na parcela de fornecimento das faturas atuais.”

Section B — Current Baseline (simple, non-technical)

Show:

“Consumo anual estimado: {MWh_annual} MWh”

“Custo anual atual (fornecimento): R$ {baseline_cost_12m}”

“Preço médio atual (fornecimento): R$ {baseline_cost_12m / MWh_annual} / MWh”

Section C — Comparison Table (core)

Each quote row shows:

supplier name

energy type

term (months)

client price (R$/MWh)

economia estimada no período (R$)

economia média por mês (R$)

short qualitative note (preset selectable, max 8–10 words)

Section D — Recommended Option

Renan selects one quote as “Recomendado” before generation.

Show:

term, client price, savings_total, savings_monthly_avg

“Por que recomendamos” (preset reasons)

Section E — Next Steps (simple)

Escolher a opção (prazo e modelo)

Assinar documentos e aprovar garantias (se aplicável)

Onboarding com a comercializadora + data de início do fornecimento

Internal vs Client Data Separation (Hard Rule)

PDF must never expose:

supplier base price

uplift/commission
Only show:

client_price_r$/mwh

derived savings values

Acceptance Criteria

A non-expert understands:

what they spend today (energy supply component)

what they could save (best option + per term)

why the recommended option is recommended

Renan generates a proposal in <2 minutes

Output increases clarity, not complexity

NOTE TO REPLIT (important implementation detail)

Please confirm how supplier quotes are structured in the database/UI to ensure term-based pricing is handled correctly.
One supplier may provide multiple prices for different terms (e.g., 12/24/36/60 months). The Proposal Generator must treat each (supplier × term) as a distinct quote row, and calculate savings using that quote’s specific term_months and client_price_r$/mwh.
