URGENT: Landing page conversion failure — /reduza
Problem
Live PPC campaign data from May 30 - June 1 shows 29 clicks, 0 form submissions, 0 conversions. ~50% of clicks are from qualified-intent searches that should have converted. The landing page is the bottleneck.
Required fixes (priority order)
Fix 1 — Make the "skip bill upload" option clearly visible (CRITICAL)
Current: Underlined purple text "Não tenho minha conta agora — quero ser contatado depois" looks like a small footnote link. Easy to miss. Users who don't have their bill in hand bounce.
Required change: Promote this to a SECOND CLEAR PATH at the same visual prominence as the bill upload.
Specifically, redesign the form's bill section to have TWO equally prominent options:
[ Option A: Upload your bill ]
   [drag-drop area]
   "Recommended — analysis in 24h"

— OR —

[ Option B: Get contacted to send later ]
   "Don't have your bill right now? Submit your details and we'll
    contact you to collect it. Analysis in 48h."
   [Continue without bill button]
Both options should be visually balanced — neither prominent over the other. Use cards or boxes with equal sizing.
When user clicks Option B, the acknowledgement checkbox appears inline (current behavior is fine), and the submit button proceeds.
Fix 2 — Reduce form length (HIGH)
Current: 9 fields = Name, Company, Email, Phone, State, City, Type of business, Average bill, Bill upload.
Required: Cut to 5 required fields for initial submission:

Full name (required)
Company name (required)
WhatsApp/Phone (required)
State (required dropdown)
Average monthly bill (required dropdown)

Move to "after submission" or make optional:

Email — collect on the /obrigado page or via the WhatsApp button
City — derive from phone area code or ask on follow-up
Type of business — ask on the /obrigado page or sales call
Bill upload — keep as optional after the core 5 fields, OR move entirely to /obrigado page as the next action

Fix 3 — Soften the R$5k qualifier (MEDIUM)
Current: "Average monthly bill amount" dropdown with text saying "We serve companies with bills starting at R$5,000/month. Smaller bills can be analyzed case-by-case basis."
Problem: This text appears BEFORE the user has invested time in the form. Many sub-R$5k or borderline businesses bail at this point thinking they don't qualify.
Required change: Remove the "R$5,000/month" qualifier from the inline form text. Keep the bill amount dropdown but with options like:

Até R$3,000 / mês
R$3,000 - R$5,000 / mês
R$5,000 - R$10,000 / mês
R$10,000 - R$30,000 / mês
Acima de R$30,000 / mês

Show the qualifier ONLY on the /obrigado page after submission (variant tipo=caso-especial which already handles sub-R$5k correctly).
This lets you capture more leads at the top of funnel, then qualify on the back end. Sub-R$5k leads go to a slower track but aren't lost.
Fix 4 — Add trust signals near the CTA (MEDIUM)
Right above the submit button, add small trust signals:

Number of customers / companies served (use a real number if you have one, or omit)
"23 estados atendidos"
"Análise por especialista — 24h úteis"
"Sem custo · Sem compromisso"

Currently these are scattered. Concentrating them near the action button reduces last-second hesitation.
Fix 5 — Above-the-fold CTA testing
Current: Hero CTA "Receive my free analysis" is a single button.
Add: Sticky scroll button or secondary CTA after the "How it works" section. Many users want to read more before committing. Multiple submission points throughout the page = higher conversion.
