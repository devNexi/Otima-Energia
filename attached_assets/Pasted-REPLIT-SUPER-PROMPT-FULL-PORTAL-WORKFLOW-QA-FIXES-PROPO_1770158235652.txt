The clean mental model (this is the key)
RFQ = outbound intent

“Here is the structured request we sent to suppliers.”

Quote = inbound reality

“Here is what suppliers actually sent back.”

They sit on opposite sides of the supplier boundary.

Why they must be separate (even if Renan never thinks about it)
1. RFQs are one-to-many

One RFQ → sent to 5, 10, 20 suppliers

Each supplier may:

respond

not respond

respond late

respond with multiple terms (24/36/60 months)

2. Quotes are many-to-one

Many quotes → belong to one RFQ

Quotes arrive:

by email

WhatsApp

PDF

Excel

portal form

Quotes are messy, inconsistent, and must be captured immutably (your Deal OS logic)

3. Operational truth

In Brazil:

You cannot assume a quote exists just because an RFQ was sent

You must track:

who received the RFQ

who replied

who ghosted

who replied out of format

who sent multiple variants

That’s impossible if RFQs and Quotes are the same object.
